  • 84% of scientists say that developing software is essential to their research. [1]
  • Incorrect publications have led to a reproducibility and credibility crisi. [2, 3]

[1] Jo Hannay et al. ‚ÄúHow Do Scientists Develop and Use Scientific Software?‚Äù
[2] Monya Baker. ‚Äú1,500 scientists lift the lid on reproducibility‚Äù.

Researchers...

  • often lack knowledge about principles and practices of the software engineering discipline. [3, 4]
  • do not gain reputation for developing software
  • are pressed to publish results as fast as possible [5]

[4] Lucas Joppa et al. ‚ÄúTroubling Trends in Scientific Software Use‚Äù.
[5] Mark De Rond and Alan N Miller. ‚ÄúPublish or perish: bane or boon of academic life?‚Äù

Consequences

  • Low code quality
  • Hard to understand
  • (maybe) neither published nor documented

What is Clean Code?

  • Term coined by Robert C. Martin
  • No strict rules, but a set of principles to make code easy to understand, extend and adapt

‚ÄúIndeed, the ratio of time spent reading versus writing is well over 10 to 1. We are constantly reading old code as part of the effort to write new code. ...[Therefore,] making it easy to read makes it easier to write.‚Äù - Robert C. Martin
